Service Innovation for Sheetzclose

Applying the knowledge and skills I have cultivated in the course Service Design, I have collaborated with 5 other students to develop an innovation for Sheetz' service. We initially proposed a solution of Biometrics platform. We aimed to enhance convenience to attach and save customer information such as credit card, ID. The rewards card would also be attached to a single fingerprint. However, after receiving criticisms from peers, the professors and the client, we decided to use E-ZPass as a new alternative solution. E-ZPass is an east coast toll route device that allows users to pay for tolls without cash or stopping at the gate. We proposed Sheetz to employ the infrastructure that E-ZPass has already created for RFID tags to provide a more seamless experience for their customers. ​Displayed on the right is our final presentation slides.

My team has elaborated on the iterations, feedback, and refinements we have gone through to arrive at the final service innovation for Sheetz in the process journal.

Mobile App for Food Trucks at SXSW Austinclose

TThrough Interactive Design Overview, I was given the very first introduction and practice of brainstorming, sketching, storyboarding, wire framing and prototyping. Based on the scenarios given, I identified problems and made use of a human-centered design approach to resolve the issue. In the first project, I collaborated with classmates to resolve the issue of festival goers wanting to get the most out of their festival experience by making it easier for them to quickly grab food in a rush from one event to the next on the schedule, without missing a thing. Displayed on the right is the presentation slides pitching our solution and walking the audience through our mobile app prototype.

My teammates and I have iterated a series of steps from initial wire framing to developing polished mobile and web demo of our mobile app. They have been illustrated and elaborated on in our process journal.
